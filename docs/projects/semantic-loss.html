Semantic Loss

Directly embedding logical constraints into loss functions

This page tracks the constraint-as-loss line in which symbolic knowledge is introduced without a teacher model. The central object is the probability mass assigned to satisfying worlds under the current output distribution.

Short overview

Semantic Loss turns a propositional constraint into a training objective by summing the probability of all satisfying assignments. The loss becomes small when the model places most of its output mass on valid structured outputs.

Key idea

A key move is to interpret logic not as a local penalty on individual variables, but as a global property of the full output space. This shifts optimization from pointwise labels to the total mass of constraint-satisfying worlds.

Mechanism

The network induces a distribution over Boolean assignments, the satisfying assignments are enumerated or compiled, and the final loss is the negative log of their total probability. The mechanism is exact at the semantic level, even though its computational tractability depends on the structure of the constraint.

Comparison with Logic-Net and DL2

Compared with Logic-Net, Semantic Loss does not construct a teacher distribution and does not distill rule bias back into parameters through a separate student-teacher loop. The constraint acts directly inside the objective.

Compared with DL2, Semantic Loss preserves an exact satisfaction-mass interpretation over Boolean worlds, whereas DL2 uses a broader declarative constraint language together with differentiable surrogates and optimization-based constraint handling.
