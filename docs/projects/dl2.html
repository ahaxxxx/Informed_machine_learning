DL2: Deep Learning with Logical Constraints

Constraint-driven optimization in neural networks

This page tracks the line in which logical or geometric constraints are handled through declarative formulations, differentiable surrogates, and optimization procedures that can be inserted into training or querying.

Problem setting

DL2 addresses settings in which a model should satisfy constraints that are not naturally expressed by ordinary supervised labels alone. The constraints may involve inputs, outputs, latent variables, or relations among multiple examples.

Core idea

The central move is to write constraints in a declarative language and then translate them into differentiable loss terms or inner optimization problems. This makes logic a programmable interface for training objectives rather than a fixed post-processing rule set.

Optimization perspective

DL2 can be read as a nested optimization framework: one part searches for violating assignments or adversarial witnesses, and another part updates model parameters so that those violations become harder to realize. Constraint handling is therefore optimization-driven rather than purely symbolic.

Comparison with Semantic Loss

Semantic Loss is most naturally read as exact probability-mass optimization over satisfying Boolean worlds. DL2 instead emphasizes a broader constraint language and differentiable surrogates that can accommodate more general numeric and geometric constraints.

One practical difference is that DL2 often exposes an explicit optimization loop over constraint violations, whereas Semantic Loss centers on the mass of valid assignments under the current distribution.
