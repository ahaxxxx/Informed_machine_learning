Research Interests

A focused map of the questions I am currently investigating

Question 01

Where does prior knowledge most effectively enter the learning pipeline?

I am interested in how the same prior knowledge changes once it is introduced through data, the hypothesis set, the learning algorithm, or the final hypothesis.

Question 02

How do different constraint mechanisms influence optimization dynamics?

A central comparison is between teacher-student regularization, semantic losses, and declarative constraints, especially in terms of optimization behavior and failure modes.

Question 03

Can the integration of prior knowledge occur earlier, for example through feature selection?

I am exploring whether prior knowledge can be built directly into feature-selection algorithm design, data representation, or the training procedure rather than only to the output stage.

Current Progress

Current research progress and next analytical steps

April 2026

Established the comparative reading baseline from the survey and Logic-Net

The current research line is now anchored in one core comparison: how prior knowledge changes once it enters optimization through posterior bias, direct loss terms, or later declarative constraints.

April 2026

Completed a first full pass of Semantic Loss

The main gain from this stage is a clearer separation between softmax class probabilities, Bernoulli world probabilities, and probability mass over satisfying worlds, which is essential for later comparison with DL2.

Current

Reading DL2 while repeatedly revisiting Semantic Loss through toy code

The present focus is to understand, at the mechanism level, how knowledge enters machine learning through surrogate losses, feasible sets, and satisfying-world mass, using repeated reading together with code-level tracing.

Next

Extend the line toward granular computing and knowledge-guided feature selection

After consolidating DL2 and Semantic Loss, the next stage is to move into three granular-computing papers and test whether prior knowledge can be injected earlier through the design of feature-selection algorithms.
