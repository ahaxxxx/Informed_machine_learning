# math.bozhanli.com Site Plan

## Positioning

This site is not a formal math textbook and not a polished cram-school portal.

It is a public reconstruction of mathematical foundations by a cross-disciplinary learner who wants to:

- rebuild calculus, linear algebra, and probability rigorously
- explain difficult ideas in plain language
- connect undergraduate foundations to advanced mathematics and machine learning

## Content layers

1. `Foundation notes`
   Explain concepts in plain language and connect them to the formal definitions.

2. `Problem-pattern notes`
   Summarize common question types, recurring tricks, and failure points.

3. `Bridge notes`
   Connect undergraduate topics to later courses such as analysis, abstract algebra, optimization, and geometry.

4. `Live Q&A archive`
   Turn public Q&A into reusable notes and FAQ entries.

## Audience

- undergraduates in economics, management, CS, AI, and related majors
- cross-disciplinary learners preparing for deeper math
- students who want understanding, not only short-term exam tricks

## Tone

- serious but not pretentious
- clear but not oversimplified
- ambitious but honest about the current learning stage
